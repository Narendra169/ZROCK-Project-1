DIY yarn wrapped key covers

Materials:

  • • Key
  • • Embroidery Floss (about 5 feet)
  • • Tacky Glue
  • • Scissors

Steps:

  1. Add a thin layer of glue to both sides of the key's bow (aka the top part). It's easier to apply a small amount of glue then add more as you go.
  2. Find the center of your embroidery floss and place it on the bottom of the key's bow.
  3. Working your way to the top, start wrapping the embroidery floss around the key cover, alternating between the two sides of the yarn. Add more glue as needed and continue wrapping.
  4. If your key has multiple holes at the top like mine, you’ll need to individually wrap around them too (see images below).
  5. Secure the first end of the embroidery floss by gluing it to the rest of the yarn. Cut any excess yarn off if needed.
  6. Wrap the remaining end around until the key is completely covered.
  7. Glue the end to secure.

iPad Case Cover Sleeve Craft Project

Materials:

  • • Self-adhesive cork roll (or regular cork roll)
  • • Recycled t-shirt or fabric
  • • Ruler
  • • Pen
  • • Scissors
  • • Threading needle
  • • Embroidery floss
  • • 2 Buttons
  • • Yarn (for closure)

Steps:

  1. Lay the cork on a flat surface with the adhesive side facing up.
  2. Place iPad on top and trace about an inch away from each side. Cut out two equal pieces, which will be the front and back of the case. For a standard iPad size, cut out two 11 in. x 9 1/4 in. pieces.
  3. Cut an additional piece for the flap.
  4. Peel away the backing from the adhesive side of the cork pieces and lay the fabric on top. Smooth out and cut away any excess pieces from the edges.
  5. Hand sew one of the buttons onto the flap with embroidery floss. Sew the other to the front base piece, making sure both buttons align perfectly.
  6. With the fabric sides facing inward, stack the front and back pieces on top of each other. Starting from one end and making your way around, stitch the two pieces together with embroidery floss, leaving one side open to insert the iPad.
  7. Stitch the flap and the back layer together.
  8. Grab your yarn and knot it onto one of the buttons to make a string-and-button closure.

Enjoy your DIY iPad case cover sleeve!

DIY Vase from an Ornament

Materials:

  • • Glass ornament (pack of 4 from Michaels for $3)
  • • Gravel or sand
  • • Fishing line
  • • Scissors

Steps:

  1. Remove the top off the ornament. Separate the cap from the hook. Make sure the ornament has a long "V" shaped wire hook as shown above. The short ones will not hold the weight of the vase.
  2. Place a little bit of gravel into the ornament (not too much or else it will get too heavy).
  3. Tie a fishing line onto the top of the hook and place the hook back into the ornament.
  4. Before adding water, dangle the ornament from the wire to ensure it holds up (place your hand underneath just in case it drops).
  5. Add water (not too much) and flowers. Again, ensure it holds up properly before hanging.

Enjoy your DIY vase made from an ornament!

DIY Wall-Mounted Shelf from a Shoebox

Materials:

  • • Shoebox or Photo Box (pick one with thick walls)
  • • Spray Paint (for the base color of the box, e.g., gold)
  • • Decorative Paper (for the inside wall of the box)
  • • Scissors
  • • Glue Stick
  • • Screwdriver and 2 Screws

Steps:

  1. Cut your decorative paper to the size of the back wall of the shoebox. Ensure it fits perfectly inside. Set aside.
  2. Spray paint the inside and outside walls of the shoebox to cover up any logos it may have.
  3. Once it is completely dry, attach the box to the wall with a screwdriver and nail. For extra sturdiness, screw another nail on the other side. Tip: Hanging the box vertically will be sturdier and less likely to cause a dip on the ledge of the shelf if you display heavier objects.
  4. Apply glue to the back side of your paper and attach it to the inside of the box. By attaching the paper once the box is already hanging on the wall, the screws will remain hidden.
  5. Display your favorite (lightweight) decors and you're set!

Enjoy your DIY wall-mounted shelf made from a shoebox!

DIY Tissue Box Flower Vase

Materials:

  • • Tissue Box
  • • Newspaper Strips
  • • 3 Disposable Cups
  • • Glossy Decoupage Medium
  • • Acrylic Paint
  • • Foam Brush
  • • Optional Add-Ons: 2 Small Cabinet Knobs
  • • Ribbon
  • • Tacky Glue (or E6000 glue for heavier knobs)

Steps:

  1. Peel off the clear plastic strip from the inside of the tissue box.
  2. Apply a coat of decoupage medium with a foam brush, add a newspaper strip on top of it, and add another coat on top of the strip. Repeat until the entire box is covered.
  3. Once dry, paint the entire box with acrylic paint.
  4. For a decorative touch, attach cabinet knobs with glue (tip: if the knobs are heavy, use rubber bands to hold in place until dry).
  5. Add ribbon around the box with tacky glue.
  6. Insert disposable cups inside the box. You may need to bend them a little, but they should fit snugly inside.
  7. Add water to the cups and arrange flowers.

Tip: When replacing flowers, first remove flowers then pull out cups one by one and dump out the water. Place cups back into the box to reuse with a new bouquet.
